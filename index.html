360 Virtual Real estate tours


Overview

By Definitionis a simulation of an existing location, usually composed of a sequence of videos or still images. It may also use other multimedia elements such as sound effects, music, narration, and text. It is distinguished from the use of live television to affect tele-tourism. in detail 3D virtual tours is the realistic 3D geovisualisation of virtual environments, which allows the user to explore physical places without physical travel. During the current pandemic, there has been a rise in 360 virutal tours in real estate since the rules and guidelines of todays pandemic restricts groups, and promotes social distancing. The real estate agency has used this time to adapt and stay a float by offering virtual tours that put the power into the customers hands and allow them to choose what they are looking at in the homes and when they are looking at the homes.

Virtual tours though, are not new , they came into focus in 1994 with the first example of the tours being a museum. This museum was of Dudley Castle in England as it was in 1550. This consisted of a computer controlled laserdisc based system designed by British-based engineer Colin Johnson. The tour was used by Queen Elizabeth II, when she officially opened the visitor centre in June 1994. Because the Queen's officials had requested titles, descriptions and instructions of all activities, the system was named and described as: "Virtual Tour, being a cross between Virtual Reality and Royal Tour." Details of the original project can be viewed online. The system was featured in a conference held by the British Museum in November 1994 and in a subsequent technical paper.

How does it work

When a user begins their tour, they will select their starting point depending on the host.. a starting point may already be selected. From there the customer will either have complete control of the tour or the user will be walked through a tour and be able to control what they are looking at. This all depends on what the host has set up for the user. If it is a self guided tour the user can enter or exit a room, spend as much time in one area of the home, and control what they are looking at. If the tour is guided the user will be lead to each room by the video but still be able to control what they are looking at and in most cases can pause the video and examin the room. This way of touring gives the users freedom to control their tours in a relaxed atmosphere!
To make a 360 virtual tour the user will begin by purchasing a camera that would suport capturing pictures or videos for the tour. Next , when a good camera is selected you can move forward and take your pictures and videos. Upside and Downside

Like anything else in life.. virutal tours has its possitives and its negatives.
- If the user us not in a location with proper internet , it could make the virtual experience unpleasant.
- If host decides to include audio with the tour, this could go good.. but at times it can cause issues for the camera and image side and it leaves room for errors.
-Because the virutal tour is a no contact method, if questions should be brought up in the process there will be no available communication with the host unless a chat or preplanned communication is in order for the user to use. -If a host does not choose the proper equipment, the tour could cause motion sickness.. dizzyness .. nauseua and just discomfort for the customer. -Because of social distancing rules in place.. virtual tours have been a great way for the real estate agency to continue their work and introduce a personal looking into the homes without people leaving their safe place. -According to the NAR (Nation Association of Realtors) buyers found the tool helpful and enjoyable. In fact 54% of those who partook in the studies wouldnt look at a location that did not have the virtual images.
