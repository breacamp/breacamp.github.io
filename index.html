360 Virtual tours
In Real Estate

Overview

In general, a Virtual Tour is a simulation of an existing location, usually composed of a sequence of videos or still images, it can also include music, narration, or any sound effect! These tours are usually made up of pictures that are taken from a still point in a spot within a room or building. The difference between a virtual tour and watching tv is that you are in control of what you are viewing and the view (in most cases are photographed or videoed from an eye level or (walking pace) to make sure that the user gets the in person feel of the experience. Businesses usually use 360-degree content to promote themselves on social media like Facebook and YouTube — and it is thriving. This trend started in 2016 and has given marketers a new way to showcase their products ever since. A study conducted by Social Bakers, they found out that 360-degree photos make companies more engaging. Business who provide virtual tours to gain more fans and sales succeed by as much as 16-67%. That being said, brands from all industries must thrive to produce and share 360-degree content for their fans online. In these times... social distancing and conducting business from a far is a key element in attracting cliental. Real estate Companies from all over had to adjust their methods and make their clients not only feel safe but also feel like they were still getting the view of the homes that they needed. To do this they turned to 360 virtual tours. These tours give the power back to the clients that the current pandemic may have taken away. This allows the user to search the rooms in the homes, look into certain amenities as if they were in person!

Where can we use it and why would we use it?

Today 360 virtual tours are on a variety of platforms... they are on common sites such as YouTube, Zillow etc... But the system is not only limited to real estate, they are found for people to view other countries! Such as the street views of Paris or Rome, they are also used for personal tours in museums that may be across the world for some people. There is a variety of places that 360 virtual tours are used, and it is not limited to just real estate tours! As mentioned above the virtual tours in today’s pandemic is a key element to attracting people and to keeping businesses afloat and still running in areas where gatherings or meetings are restricted. According to a market research that was conducted by a independent firm and google- They found that a business listing with a virtual tour is twice as likely to generate interest online. It was also said that people who go on these virtual tours tend to go to their associated websites which lead to onsite visits if permitted as well as meetings to discuss deals. It was also mentioned that people who are in the market for homes appreciate the ability to participate in virtual tours and half of them would prefer that businesses go the way of this modern-day marketing material. What can go wrong?

There is a number of ways that hosting or participating in a virtual tour could go wrong.. These include


  • Mis-representation


    Some companies may not have a proper idea of what a virtual tour or 360 tour is. To some people these tours may mean a simple video of the location, a pictured slide show. Or companies may not get the full view of the home ruining the experience for the user because they may not be able to 1. take control of what they see 2. Or see what they were wanting to.. especiall in a pictured slide show.
  • Companies not meeting the basic Qualifications to be a successful Virtual tour
    • If a company does not give the view control to their users this could leave the users feeling fustrated and not give them a accurate view of the home.. leading them to seek out other locations.
    • If a company uses anything other than real photos of the property , the tour can come off as cartoonish and leave the users un interested and also not give a valid and true representation of the home.
    • If a site or a tour is not able to be access from both a computer AND a mobile device this could greatly reduce the attention the homes get since, a large percentage of home buyers are using websites as first point of contact when searching for a home! a study showed that 73% used mobile or tablet search device vs the those who did not and 93% used online websited vs those who did not! link to study
    The site should be easily accessible wether through mobile or website
  • The tour not maintaning or having proper lighting in the home and include different views as well as the outside views (which are mistakenly left out)
  • The company not acquiring the proper hardware to conduct the tour, poor quality can lead to a user feeling light headed .. dizzy, and nauseaus
